### 2.2 The fairness oracle, and the number that shapes the generator

`tools/map-rules.js` (352 lines) is a pure window-IIFE exposing `CTD3MapRules.check({path, buildSlots, castle}) → [{code, message, severity}]` over ten warn-tier rules (H1, H2, H3, H4, H5, H7, H8, H10, H11, H12), plus `WORLD_HALF_X=14`, `WORLD_HALF_Z=9`, `T1_RANGE=7.5` and the geometry helpers `dist, segDist, segments, pathLength, corners, samplePath, edgeGap` — exported, per its own header, *"so the generator can reuse the SAME definitions the oracle judges by rather than reimplementing them."* It never throws on a partial map and catches per-rule exceptions so a generate-and-reject loop still gets the other nine verdicts.

The pinned official-map baseline, from this session's test run:

| Map | Oracle warns |
|---|---|
| plains | H1, H2, H5 |
| forest | H1, H5, H7 |
| mountain | H1, H7 |
| riverbend | H1, H5 |
| tidewater | H1 |
| snowfall_pass | H7 |

**No shipped map is oracle-clean.** The worst carries three warns, the best carries one, and H1 (inside-corner slot) fires on five of six. This is the empirical acceptance threshold the generate-and-reject loop must be calibrated against — demanding zero warns without evidence risks a loop that never converges, while accepting three warns ships maps no better than the weakest hand-authored one. It also carries a genuinely favourable implication: **H1, H5, H11 and H12 are slot-*placement* rules, which a generator controls completely.** A generator that places slots deliberately should beat every hand-authored map on the oracle. That is the differentiation bet's strongest technical argument and it is why §3(a) is build-now.

### 2.3 The map schema is flat — which settles a known-unknown for free

The dispatch asks the generator spec to either bound generated terrain height or answer the decal question (sprint 5's known-unknown: *"a map with substantial elevation could fragment a large ring into arcs… this becomes a real question the moment the layout generator starts producing terrain"*).

**It is bounded by construction, and the evidence is that the schema has no elevation field at all.** A map object is `{id, displayName, …, theme, castle: {x, y, z}, path: [{x,z}…], buildSlots: [{id,x,z}…], waves: […]}`. `path` waypoints and `buildSlots` carry **only x and z**. The single `y` in every official map is `castle.y`, and it is `0` in all six (`maps.js` lines 138, 198, 261, 322, 382, 440). The 0.2-tall path tiles that buried the decals are a *renderer* constant in `scene.js`, not authored data. A generator that emits the existing schema therefore **cannot** produce elevation, cannot fragment a decal ring, and needs no decal answer. D14 records this as a deliberate bound rather than an accident, so a future elevation feature inherits the open question explicitly instead of silently.

### (c) Endless leaderboard — **BUILD NOW, restructured**, chunks T-2 and T-3

The dispatch asks for this to be adjudicated with the RTDB dependency explicit. Doing so surfaced that **the dependency has been partly misdiagnosed for three sprints.**

Sprint 4 recorded that publishing a community map was *"denied to this session by the console-managed RTDB rules (`PERMISSION_DENIED` on write)"*, and sprints 5 and 6 carried that forward as "blocked on an operator Firebase visit." Two facts now contradict the diagnosis:

1. **ADR-017's published rules already permit the write.** Under `ctd3-community`, `$code` carries `".write": "auth != null"` with a 4-uppercase-letter code-shape validation. A community publish from an authenticated client is allowed today.
2. **The auth was the thing failing, not the rules.** ADR-017 §"referrer restrictions" states that the API-key restriction gates Identity Toolkit — the call `signInAnonymously()` makes — while RTDB REST requests carry an auth token instead and are *not* gated by it. Commit `0b67b8f` records the operator's own finding verbatim: a dev server on a non-allowlisted port *"throws `auth/requests-from-referer-http://localhost:<port>-are-blocked` at the gateway — it reads like a rules/`PERMISSION_DENIED` denial but is not."* Sprint 4 and sprint 6 both served on `:3004`, which is the Lookout tunnel's port and is **not** on the Firebase allowlist. Anonymous auth fails → `auth == null` → the RTDB write is correctly denied → the symptom reads as a rules gap.

So the community-publish blocker is very likely **not real**, and dissolves by serving on `localhost:3030` (now allowlisted) or from the live site. That is chunk **T-2**, an S: re-run the publish and the live-Community endless verification that R-2 was blocked on for two sprints.

**The `ctd3-scores` dependency is real but is not a blocker on engineering.** The RTDB root is `".read": false, ".write": false`, so a new top-level path genuinely needs a published rules block. But ADR-017's own §"Updating the rules" names **the ADR as the canonical source and the console as the executing copy**, with the documented procedure being "edit the rules block in the ADR, then paste it into the console." A worker session can therefore do everything except the paste: build the client, author the exact rules JSON into ADR-017, and degrade gracefully (local-only personal best, leaderboard panel hidden or showing an honest "not yet published" state) until the operator pastes. That is chunk **T-3**. The operator's action shrinks from "unblock this work" to "one paste that switches it on."

**ADR-017's forgeability caveat carries and must be restated in the UI's framing:** anonymous auth with no server-side validation means a leaderboard is forgeable by anyone with devtools. ADR-017 ratified "personal-site scale of trust" for `ctd3-community`; the leaderboard inherits that stance. It is an ornament, not a competitive ladder, and T-3's copy should not imply otherwise.

### (e) Tower-roster depth / combination mechanic — **DEFER once more, with a named unblock that actually arrives this cycle**

Parked in ADR-036 and again in ADR-037 with the reason "competes with endless for the same cycle." **That reason is now spent** — endless has shipped — so it needs a real verdict rather than a third repetition, as the dispatch demands.

The verdict is defer, but the argument has inverted, and the new evidence is worth recording because it is the first *empirical* case for tower depth this project has had. Endless surfaced a **late-run decision vacuum**. `endless.js`'s own comment explains why `INTEREST_CAP` exists: uncapped interest compounds without limit *"once a build fills every slot and stops spending"* — measured at 45.7 % of all income on a long run. That sentence is a balance note, but it is also a design finding: **in a 26-wave run the player exhausts the build space and the remaining decisions collapse to "bank or call early."** Four towers × three tiers is enough decision surface for eight waves and demonstrably not enough for twenty-six. Cycle 1 gave the economy runway; cycle 2 gave the run length; the build is now the shortest pole.

It is nonetheless deferred, for one reason: it is an XL design problem (ETD's element lattice is an entire game system) and this cycle already carries an XL in the generator. Stacking two unbuilt XLs is precisely the error D10 correctly refused. **Named unblock, and it is not vague:** T-1's calibration emits multi-seed endless curve CSVs; from those, identify the wave index at which marginal build decisions stop changing run depth. That number is the scope input for a combination mechanic — it says how much decision surface is missing and where. It arrives this cycle, so cycle 4 can adjudicate this with data instead of instinct.

## 6. Decisions

**Amendment ledger, stated up front so no amendment is made silently.** ADR-036 **D1–D3, D5 and D6 stand unchanged**; **D4 is clarified, not overturned, by D21**. ADR-037 **D8, D10 and D12 stand unchanged**; **D7 is amended by D19** (deploy-as-gate → deploy-as-ritual, with a restore condition), **D9 is amended by D21** (two parameters reclassified from decided values to calibration starting points), and **D11 is amended by D16** (calibration split; its human gate demoted to non-blocking). An earlier draft of this ADR described D7 as merely "spent" while replacing it — that is an amendment and is labelled as one here.

**On ADR-036 D6 and the new selectable.** D6 requires every selectable mode or map to keep an automated selection→effect path. T-6 adds a new selectable ("Random Map") whose own completion is browser-observable rather than harness-asserted, so **D6 is discharged by T-5 rather than by T-6**: T-5's 50-seed batch asserts in Node that every generated map is structurally valid, oracle-clean to a measured bar, and completable under a scripted build — a stronger guarantee than a single per-map check. Recorded explicitly so D6 is not left silently unaddressed.

- **D13 — The layout generator is specced here, and this discharges D10.** Shape: a **seeded, deterministic, generate-and-reject** pipeline emitting the existing map-object schema. *Generation* proceeds in two stages so it can be dispatched as two chunks: (i) a rectilinear path plus castle terminus, and (ii) build-slot placement. *Rejection* runs two oracles in ascending cost order — `CTD3MapRules.check()` first (pure, microseconds), then `CTD3SimCore.runScripted()` for completability (a full sim) — so cheap rejections happen before expensive ones. **Determinism:** one seed produces one byte-identical map object, using the same seeded-RNG idiom `endless.js` already carries — note that `rngFrom` **is** exported on `window.CTD3Endless` but `mixSeed` is **not**, so reuse `rngFrom` and reimplement `mixSeed`'s one-line mixing idiom rather than importing it — never `Math.random()`; this is what makes the cycle-4 daily challenge a composition rather than a rewrite, and it is harness-assertable by deep-equality across two generations. **Acceptance threshold, anchored to measurement rather than aspiration (§2.2):** target **zero** oracle warns; accept **≤1** warn after the attempt budget is spent; **never emit a map carrying more warns than the worst shipped map (3)**, and assert over a K-seed batch that the generated mean warn count **beats the official-map mean (2.00** — plains 3, forest 3, mountain 2, riverbend 2, tidewater 1, snowfall_pass 1, summing to 12 over six maps**)**. Rationale for a band rather than a hard zero: no hand-authored map is oracle-clean, so a hard zero is unevidenced and risks non-convergence, while the H1/H5/H11/H12 placement rules that dominate the official warn set are exactly the ones a generator controls — so beating the baseline is the honest bar and is expected to be comfortably met. **Bounded attempts with a deterministic fallback:** a fixed attempt budget per stage, and on exhaustion the loop returns its best-scoring candidate rather than looping forever or throwing — a generator that can hang is not shippable behind a UI button. **Structural constraints inherited, not re-derived:** paths are axis-aligned (the editor's `AXIS` blocker), every segment ≥3 units (`H8a`), the castle sits at the path's end (`H9`), no slot on the path (`H6`), slots on integer coordinates (`SLOT_INT`), at least one slot (`NO_SLOTS`), within `WORLD_HALF_X=14` / `WORLD_HALF_Z=9`. **W8's 35-concurrent ceiling is satisfied by construction and must not be re-implemented:** the generator produces *layouts*, never waves; in endless, waves come from `CTD3Endless.waveFor()`, whose `SPAWN_CEILING = 35` is already harness-asserted (`endless-spawn-bound`, measured peak 22).

- **D14 — Generated maps are flat. Terrain elevation is explicitly out of scope, and this bounds the decal question.** The map schema carries no height field: path waypoints and build slots are `{x, z}` only, and `castle.y` is `0` on all six official maps (§2.3). A generator emitting this schema cannot produce elevation and therefore cannot fragment a large decal ring into arcs. Sprint 5's known-unknown — *"the range circle over genuinely tall terrain"* — is hereby **deferred to any future elevation feature rather than to this generator**, and any such feature inherits it as an explicit precondition: it must settle the `GROUND_DECAL_Y` clearance answer before shipping. Recording it this way prevents the unknown from being silently discharged by a generator that merely happened not to trigger it.

- **D15 — Generated maps enter through a "Random Map" card, ephemerally, endless-only.** They are *not* a fourth map-select tab (tab churn for a single entry) and endless is *not* forced into map-per-run (which would remove replaying a map you liked). **A generated map needs a new registration path in `maps.js`, and this is a deliberate exception to "reuse the existing seam."** The seam community maps use is `window.CTD3Maps.saveUserMap(combined)`, which ends in `persistUserMaps()` and therefore **writes `localStorage['ctd3:userMaps']`** — the opposite of what an ephemeral map needs; and the underlying `_ingestMap` is a private function inside the `maps.js` IIFE that no external caller can reach. Since `startMap()` resolves through `window.CTD3Maps.byId`, which searches only `OFFICIAL_MAPS` and `USER_MAPS`, a generated map must be registered somewhere `byId` can see it **without** touching persistence: add a narrow exported `registerEphemeralMap(combined)` (in-memory only, no `persistUserMaps()` call, cleared on reload). Generated maps take a reserved **`user:gen:<seed>`** id — the `user:` prefix is load-bearing, not cosmetic, because `totalStars()` in `game.js` skips only ids beginning `user:` when summing campaign stars, so a bare `gen:` id that ever reached `ctd3:scores` would be counted as an official map and inflate unlock totals. They are **never persisted to `ctd3:userMaps`** unless the player explicitly saves one (that save path is cycle-4 work, §5). Endless-only because the campaign's authored wave/reward curves are map-specific and D18 forbids touching them; a generated map has no campaign waves to run. The run's seed must remain visible on the results screen — C-2 already surfaces it, and D13's determinism makes it meaningful.

- **D16 — AMENDS D11. Endless calibration is split: the harness half is ungated, and the feel half becomes a non-blocking post-hoc check.** D11 held that *"C-3, which sets the numbers a human will feel, is gated on operator playtest G-1,"* on the principle that unverified feel must not be stacked on unverified feel. That principle is retained; its implementation failed. Three sprints produced no playtest, and §1 establishes the cause is the gate's shape — the operator authored G-1's setup guide (`dd02560`) and still did not play. **Amendment:** (i) everything in C-3 that a harness can decide runs now as **T-1**, ungated, including the run-duration band asserted in **sim-seconds**, which measures directly what D11-era C-3's 15–35 wave band was proxying for (§2.1); (ii) **G-1 is retired and replaced by G-2**, a post-hoc validation on the *calibrated* build, which blocks nothing — the numbers stand unless the operator contradicts them. Rationale beyond the deadlock: G-1 asked the operator to feel the *campaign retune* in order to release *endless constants*, which is two removes from the decision; G-2 asks them to feel the actual artifact. Risk accepted and named: calibrated-but-unfelt endless numbers — the same risk the project already carries on the campaign retune, knowingly, across two cycles.

- **D17 — The leaderboard ships client-first; the operator's console paste is the last mile, not a prerequisite.** ADR-017 names the ADR as the canonical rules source and the console as the executing copy, so a worker session authors the exact `ctd3-scores` rules JSON *into ADR-017* and builds a client that **degrades gracefully** — local personal best works, the leaderboard surface states honestly that it is not yet published — until the paste happens. The `ctd3-community` write path is separately believed unblocked already (§3c) and T-2 confirms it. **ADR-017's forgeability stance carries unchanged**: anonymous auth plus no server-side validation means the board is an ornament at personal-site trust, and the UI copy must not imply a competitive ladder.

- **D18 — Campaign balance stays frozen through cycle 3** (extending D12). No edits to `maps.js` wave compositions or rewards, no `ENEMIES` hp/bounty, no `TOWERS` tier stats, no star thresholds, no `unlockRequirement`. The six campaign CSVs must show **no content change** at every chunk boundary. Endless constants in `endless.js` are explicitly *outside* this freeze and are T-1's subject. `wfc-rules.js` palette work (T-8) is also outside it — WFC is decorative fill consumed by `scene.js` and by no simulation path — but T-8 must run `node tools/wfc-test.cjs` because that suite carries themed-palette assertions.

- **D19 — AMENDS D7. Deploy is an operator ritual, not a gate.** D7's text makes deploy a standing precondition — pushed and cache-purged *before build chunks are dispatched* — so retiring it is an amendment, not the expiry of a one-shot condition. D7 was written against a 23-commit backlog; the backlog cleared and cycle 2 both shipped and deployed, with the final three commits pushed unprompted. Re-imposing a gate would spend the cycle's scarce gate budget on the operator behaviour that has demonstrably corrected itself. **Recommended ritual, recorded as a process note:** at every hand-back, `git push origin main`, purge the changed asset URLs via the Cloudflare dashboard's Custom Purge (no CF API token on this box; dash-session `fetch` writes 403), then `curl`-verify one changed byte is actually live before writing any "deployed" claim. If two consecutive hand-backs ship undeployed, cycle 4 should restore the gate.

- **D20 — Tower-roster depth is deferred with a data-defined unblock, not re-parked.** The "competes with endless" reason is spent. The new reason is XL-collision with the generator, and the unblock is specific: T-1's multi-seed endless CSVs identify the wave index beyond which marginal build decisions stop changing run depth. Cycle 4 adjudicates this with that number in hand.

- **D21 — AMENDS D9 (and clarifies D4). The endless interest rate and the bounty gap are calibration starting points, not decided values.** D9 states *"Bounty scales alongside at `ENDLESS_GROWTH / 1.07`"* and *"Pure interest at 2 %/15 s"*, quoting D4's *"Pure 2 %/15 s interest is reserved for endless mode where waves auto-send."* Read literally, those are decisions, and T-1 is authorised to move both — so the reclassification must be explicit rather than left for a commit to contradict silently. Three things support it: `endless.js`'s own header already describes every constant in the file as a starting value *"taken from the Element TD reference… not from CTD play,"* with C-3 named as the pass that "replaces them with measured values"; ADR-037 already treats the sibling constant this way (`ENDLESS_GROWTH` "start ≈ 1.15, calibrated by C-3"); and the sprint-4 hand-back records the rate-versus-cap question as explicitly owned by the calibration pass. **What is amended:** the *numeric values* `0.02`/`15 s` and the `/1.07` divisor become T-1's to measure. **What is not:** D4's structural decision that pure interest lives in endless and not in the campaign — that is untouched, as is D9's requirement that bounty growth trail HP growth to preserve a compounding gap. T-1 may change the size of that gap; it may not remove it or move interest into the campaign.

### Adversarial review — what it caught, and what is noted rather than fixed

A fresh-context general-purpose subagent reviewed this ADR against ADR-036, ADR-037, ADR-017, the three cycle-2 hand-backs, current source and its own live command runs, with no chat history.

**It independently confirmed the load-bearing claims**, including several this plan's conclusions rest on entirely: the empty deploy queue and the live leak fix (`git ls-remote` matching HEAD, `GROUND_DECAL_Y = 0.24` and the dispose pass present in the served `scene.js`); the harness at 73 pass with the 58 + 12 + 3 decomposition counted from output; the six-map oracle baseline per map; **the flat map schema** (six `y:` occurrences in `maps.js`, all `castle.y: 0`, at the exact lines cited — D14's entire premise); `map-rules.js`'s export surface and the verbatim header quotation; ADR-017 already permitting `ctd3-community` writes and gating only Identity Toolkit by referrer (§3c and D17); `SPAWN_CEILING = 35` and its harness assertion; `runScripted`'s map-object signature; the 20-commit count and every group hash; and the §3 staleness sweep, which it re-ran cold and found honest — nothing proposed here already exists. It also confirmed the two known gate traps are cleanly avoided: **no multi-file `git diff -- a.csv b.csv` pathspec form appears anywhere**, and **no gate asserts on a materials counter**.

**Seven MAJOR findings were fixed in this text before commit.** (1) The official-map oracle mean was stated as **1.83**; the correct figure is **2.00** (3+3+2+2+1+1 = 12 over six maps) — corrected at all four sites, including two completion conditions that would have contradicted a worker's own re-derivation. (2) **T-6 was self-defeating**: it named `_ingestMap` as the ingestion seam, but that function is private to the `maps.js` IIFE and the exported path community maps actually use (`saveUserMap`) calls `persistUserMaps()` and *writes* `ctd3:userMaps` — which T-6's own completion condition forbids. (3) **Two elevation gates could not pass**: T-7 and T-10 asserted that *every* `RingGeometry` in the scene sits at 0.24, but `syncEffects` builds transient splash rings at **y = 0.1** and the place-here disc sits at **0.25** — both correct and intentional. (4) The cycle-2 chunk arithmetic was wrong (12 of 15 / 31-for-31) and contradicted its own itemization one clause later; corrected to **9 of 13 this cycle, 28 of 32 across both**, and the §7 capacity derivation was re-based on it. (5) **D7 was declared "unchanged" and replaced in the same paragraph** — D7's text is a standing precondition, so D19 is an amendment and is now labelled one, alongside a full amendment ledger at the head of §6. (6) **T-1 was authorised to change values ADR-036 D4 and ADR-037 D9 state as decided** (2 %/15 s interest, the 1.07 bounty gap) under a document claiming those decisions stood; **D21** now reclassifies those two numbers as calibration starting points while explicitly preserving D4's structural placement of interest in endless and D9's requirement that a compounding gap exist.
