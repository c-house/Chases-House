# ADR-017: Uno

**Status**: Implemented (solo mode v1; couch hot-seat deferred to a v1.1 ADR)
**Date**: 2026-04-24 (planning) · 2026-04-25 (implementation)

## Summary

Recreate Mattel's Uno (1971) as a browser card game at [games/uno/](../../games/uno/). v1 ships **solo vs AI** and **local couch hot-seat** — the same two-pillar model Pac-Man (ADR-014) used to keep the static-site stack honest. Online multiplayer, daily seeds, and themed decks are explicitly parked for a v2 ADR. The signature feel is the **card slap**: a weighty thwock, brief hit-pause, and color-burst that turns every play into a felt event. Visuals deliberately import Uno's saturated four-color palette inside a chases.house felt-table frame — the contrast is the visual hook.

## §0 Canonical Reference — What IS Uno?

- **Genre & lineage:** Shedding-type card game in the **Crazy Eights / Mau-Mau family** (mid-1900s German trick-takers). Created 1971 by Merle Robbins, acquired by International Games (1981), now Mattel. Direct descendants: Phase 10, Skip-Bo.
- **Core loop:** Each turn, play a card matching the discard pile's **color, number, or symbol** — or draw one — racing to empty your hand first while sabotaging opponents with action cards.
- **Win/lose/score:** Round ends when one player empties their hand. Winner scores the sum of all cards left in opponents' hands (numbers = face value, action cards = 20, wild cards = 50). Match continues until a player reaches 500 (classic) or a fixed round count.
- **Iconic moments (non-negotiable):**
  1. Calling "Uno!" with one card left — and the gotcha when you forget
  2. Dropping a Wild Draw Four on the leader the turn before they go out
  3. The Skip → Reverse → Skip → Reverse runaround in 2-player
  4. Going out on a perfectly placed +2 / +4 for max points
  5. The "color called" reveal after a Wild lands
- **Subtle rules amateur clones botch:**
  - Wild Draw Four is **only legal when no other card matches the current color** (Mattel's "challenge rule"). Most house games skip it. v1 ships the official rule with a toggle.
  - Reverse in 2-player effectively acts as a Skip — same player goes again.
  - Drawing a card you can play: official rule lets you play it immediately; many house rules force you to keep it. Toggle.
  - **Stacking +2 / +4** is **not** in official rules but is the most common house rule (~85% of players). Toggle, default ON.
  - First card flipped at start of round: if Wild, reshuffle; if action card, takes effect on first player.
  - "Uno" must be declared before your second-to-last card touches the pile, or any opponent who catches you before the next play makes you draw 2.
  - Going out on an action card: in official rules, the next player still suffers the effect, but the round ends.
- **Era / look:** **Late-1970s commercial graphic-design language.** Saturated four-color palette (red / yellow / green / blue), bold geometric numerals, oblique italic "UNO" wordmark. Saul Bass meets a 1970s game-show splash.

## §1 Fit for chases.house

- **Why this game:** The gallery has Jeopardy (multiplayer trivia), Pac-Man (couch arcade), Chess (deep strategy). Uno is the canonical **card-game gap**. Card games engage a distinct cognitive register — hand management, bluffing, probabilistic risk — and globally familiar so onboarding is "it's Uno."
- **Solo / multi:** Both. Solo = vs AI. Multi-player exists in two flavors: couch hot-seat (v1) and online (v2).
- **Session length:** A round = 3–7 minutes. A full match to 500 = 20–40 minutes. v1 default is single round with optional "play match" continuation.
- **Dealbreakers:** Hidden-information networking is the only static-site complication. The fair compromise is host-authoritative state (one client owns the deck, reveals only public state) — same trust model as Jackbox/UNO Online. Defer to v2 to keep v1 small.

Local file `ai.js` mirroring Pac-Man / Chess convention.

## §3 Controls & Input

Card games are mouse/touch-native. Keyboard and gamepad are equally first-class.

- **Mouse / touch (primary):** Click/tap a card to play. Wild plays open a four-way color picker. Click the deck to draw. Long-press a card to peek (mobile zoom). Drag-to-discard is a delight option, click-to-play is the floor.
- **Keyboard:** ← / → scroll hand cursor, Enter/Space play, D draw, U call Uno, Esc menu. Number keys 1–9 jump to that card index.
- **Gamepad** (via [games/shared/gamepad.js](../../games/shared/gamepad.js)): D-pad ←/→ scroll, A play, B draw, X call Uno, Y menu. **`SharedGamepad` currently exposes only `A` and `DPAD_*`** (per ADR-016, "the other 11 standard-mapping indices land when a consumer needs them"). This ADR is the trigger to add `B`, `X`, `Y` to the shared utility — a small surface change with a real second consumer.
- **Mobile:** Hand fans across the bottom; opponents collapse to a top row; "UNO!" floating button appears when hand size hits 2 (anticipatory press window).
- **Accessibility:** Cards already carry number/symbol overlays — colorblind-safe by design. Reduced-motion variant skips card-flight animations; large-font toggle.
- **Input feel:** 250ms card-fly to pile; 40ms hit-pause on landing; 400ms "Uno-callable" window after a 2-card hand is created during which any opponent can punish. Hover/focus card lifts 8px with a soft shadow.

## §4 UI/UX Structure

1. **Title** — Animated "UNO" wordmark, big PLAY button. Below: New Game / Continue / How to Play / Settings. Idle attract: cards float into a slow shuffle every 12s.
2. **Mode/player select** — Solo (AI count + difficulty) / Couch (2–4 seats with name entry). House-rules toggles (stacking, jump-in, 7-0, force-play, draw-then-play, +4 challenge). Match length: "play to N points" or "first out wins."
3. **Active play HUD** — Center: discard pile (top card large) + draw pile. Bottom: your hand (fanned). Perimeter: opponents (avatar + card-back count + Uno-pip). Top bar: turn-direction arrow, current color bar, deck count, scoreboard.
4. **Pause** — Esc overlay: Resume / How to Play / Quit. No mid-round saves on v1 outside auto-resume.
5. **Round end** — Reveal all opponent hands; tally animates onto scoreboard. "Next round" auto-focused.
6. **Match end** — Winner card + confetti-equivalent + final scoreboard. "Play again" / "Back to gallery."
7. **High scores** — Local leaderboard (localStorage). Stats: fastest round, longest streak, biggest single-round haul, lifetime games.
8. **How to play** — Modal: card legend (every card type illustrated), three-step rules, link to house-rules toggle.

Each screen: one obvious primary CTA, Esc/back always present, layout reflows to portrait on phones.

## §5 Progression & Persistence

- **High scores:** localStorage `uno_highscores_v1`. Fastest round (ms), longest win streak, biggest haul, lifetime wins.
- **Save/resume:** Mid-round JSON snapshot to localStorage; "Continue" appears on title if a round is in progress. Cheap to implement, cheap to maintain.
- **Unlockables:** None v1.
- **Stats / achievements:** Park for v2 — YAGNI.
- **Daily seed:** **v2 stretch.** Shared seeded shuffle so every visitor faces the same deal & AI lineup; shareable result string ("UNO 2026-04-24 — 3:42, 7 left in opponents' hands"). Strong feature, but adds a seedable PRNG and a share-grid renderer — defer.

## §6 Difficulty & Game Feel

- **Difficulty curve:** Three AI tiers, no rubber-banding (shuffle variance is the natural equalizer).
- **Onboarding:** First solo run pops a 3-step coach overlay (1: match color or number, 2: action cards, 3: tap UNO when one card left). Stored in localStorage; never repeats unless reset.
- **Feedback loops** — micro-rewards firing on:
  - Card play: subtle gamepad rumble + thwip + thock + 40ms hit-pause + pile-tilt randomization
  - +2 / +4: stamp animation flying to victim's card stack
  - Wild: 400ms radial color-wash from the pile
  - Reverse: arrow sweep + vinyl-scratch sting
  - Skip: woodblock thunk
  - "UNO!": player avatar lights up with the wordmark briefly
  - Round-end: confetti from winner's avatar
- **Signature juice (the one thing that has to be right):** **The card slap.** Three audio layers (lift whoosh → mid-air thwip → landing thock) over a 250ms slide-and-tilt. If the slap isn't satisfying, the game isn't fun.

## §7 Audio

- **Music:** Off by default. Optional lounge-lo-fi loop. Persisted mute.
- **SFX inventory:** Card lift / slap / draw, Wild chime, Reverse vinyl-scratch, Skip woodblock, +2 / +4 escalating sting, menu hover/confirm/back, round-win fanfare, match-win cheer, lose drone, "UNO!" stinger.
- **Source:** **CC0 only** — Pac-Man precedent (ADR-014). Cards SFX from CC0 playing-card packs on OpenGameArt; menu SFX from Kenney UI Audio; chime synthesized via Web Audio (cheap, fits palette).
- **Voice:** No vocal "UNO!" v1 (CC0 vocal samples are scarce and licensing fragile). Stinger only; vocal in v2.
- **Mute:** Separate music + SFX sliders, persisted.
- **First-interaction gate:** Audio context unlocks on first card click in title screen.

## §8 Visual Design Direction — Brief for `/frontend-design`

> Hand this section verbatim to the `/frontend-design` skill. Do not sketch components here.

- **Mood:** Punchy, glossy, slappy, candy-poster, 70s-corporate. Bold, graphic, retro-pop, slightly over-saturated, optimistic.
- **Era / reference:** Late-1970s commercial graphic design — saturated rectangles, oblique italic display lettering, generous deep negative space (we're a dark-theme site). Saul Bass meets a 70s game-show splash.
- **Palette hints:** Uno's four canonical card colors are non-negotiable for **card faces** (saturated red / yellow / green / blue, target around `#E53935` / `#FDD835` / `#43A047` / `#1E88E5` — `/frontend-design` to refine within Uno's brand). The site frame uses the standard tokens (`--bg-deep`, gold/ember/terracotta accents). **The card palette deliberately contrasts with the site palette — that contrast IS the visual hook.** Justification: the cards must read instantly as Uno; the felt-table frame is the bridge.
- **Typography:** Card numerals get a heavy oblique display weight (Fraunces with high WONK + extreme opsz, or a free pixel-display alt — `/frontend-design` to pick). Site UI keeps Fraunces / Bricolage. The "UNO" wordmark is its own oblique italic display face.
- **Motion language:** Snappy with weight. Cards lift with a bouncy `cubic-bezier(0.2, 0.9, 0.3, 1.3)`; landing has 40ms hit-pause; reverse direction sweep is 600ms; Wild color-change is a 400ms radial wash.
- **Iconography:** Cards rendered as styled DOM (CSS + inline SVG for action symbols), not images — keeps the static stack honest, supports a11y. Card backs use the classic black / red-oval / white-italic-UNO motif, recolored slightly to harmonize with the site frame.
- **Backgrounds:** Felt-table feel — subtle radial green-to-darker gradient at the table center. No parallax. Faint card-shadow vignette focuses the eye on the discard pile.
- **Hero frame to design toward:** Discard pile center, top card a Wild reading "+4" mid color-burst, three opponent avatars semi-circled above with card-back fans, our hand fanned at the bottom showing a single yellow 7 (uno-call moment), the floating "UNO!" button glowing.

## §9 Architecture & Code Plan — Brief for `/feature-dev:feature-dev`

> Hand this section verbatim to the `/feature-dev:feature-dev` skill. Do not commit to file-level structure here.

- **Proposed files in `games/uno/`:** `index.html`, `game.js` (state machine + rules), `ai.js` (bot strategies), `render.js` (DOM/CSS), `input.js` (mouse/keyboard/gamepad routing), `audio.js` (Web Audio bus + sample loading). Six files matches Pac-Man's structure ([games/pacman/](../../games/pacman/) — `game/render/input/audio/ai/modes`).
- **Module pattern:** `window.UnoGame`, `window.UnoAI`, `window.UnoRender`, `window.UnoInput`, `window.UnoAudio`, IIFE-wrapped. Matches site convention.
- **State shape:** Explicit state machine — `TITLE → SETUP → DEALING → PLAYER_TURN → AWAITING_COLOR → AWAITING_CHALLENGE → ROUND_END → MATCH_END`. Single state object: `{ phase, players[], hands[][], discard[], drawPile[], direction, currentColor, turnIndex, unoCalls{}, scores[], rules, seed }`.
- **Render approach:** **DOM + CSS transforms.** Card count is small (108-card deck, max ~30 visible at once). DOM gives accessibility, CSS animations, and hover/focus for free. Canvas would be overkill.
- **Tick loop:** Event-driven, no rAF main loop. AI delays via `setTimeout`; animations via CSS transitions. Determinism: seedable PRNG (Mulberry32) so future daily-seed mode is forward-compatible.
- **Shared utilities:**
  - **Use** [games/shared/gamepad.js](../../games/shared/gamepad.js).
  - **Extend it:** add `B`, `X`, `Y` to `SharedGamepad.BUTTONS`. ADR-016 explicitly anticipates this ("the other 11 standard-mapping indices land when a consumer needs them"). Land the change in `games/shared/gamepad.js` as part of this implementation.
  - **Do NOT** preemptively extract a shared localStorage helper — Snake / Sudoku / Crossword each roll their own and the duplication isn't real today (DRY is "twice", not "thrice with bespoke shapes"). Re-evaluate if Uno makes it the fourth.
- **Networking:** None v1. v2 ADR adds Firebase (host-authoritative).
- **Testing (Chrome DevTools MCP, per CLAUDE.md):** `navigate_page` → `/games/uno/`, `wait_for` title, `take_snapshot`, click PLAY, click a hand card, verify discard pile updates and hand-size decrements, `list_console_messages types: ["error"]` clean, `list_network_requests` shows zero Firebase / 4xx / 5xx, `resize_page` to 375×812 portrait, re-screenshot.

## §11 Fun Audit

1. **10-second hook:** First card slap — heavy thwock, hit-pause, color radiates. Sells the game before the rules do.
2. **Skill ceiling:** Card-counting for the +4 challenge; resource-managing wilds; predicting the leader's hoarded color and color-blocking. Visible novice/expert gap.
3. **Story you tell a friend:** *"I had ONE card left, they +4'd me, I drew six in a row, ended on a +2 myself for the W."* If a round can't generate a sentence like that, the loop isn't working.
4. **Surprise:** First time the hot-seat curtain reveals the next player's hand fresh. First time a Wild Draw Four is challenged successfully. First time the color-bar rotates around the discard pile after a Wild.
5. **Restart friction:** Round-end → confetti → "Next round" auto-focused → 2 seconds. Match-end → "Play again same lineup" → 2 seconds. Ruthless.

## §12 Beauty Audit

- **Hero frame:** Discard pile center-stage with a Wild "+4" mid color-burst, three opponent avatars semi-circled above, player hand fanned at the bottom holding a single yellow 7 (uno-call moment), "UNO!" button glowing.
- **Negative space:** Generous green felt around the pile; air between cards in the player's fan.
- **Readability under pressure:** Numerals huge and bold, color-fields large, opponent card-counts in big numerals not pip stacks. Current color = a lit-up bar around the discard pile (unmissable even on fast plays).
- **Cohesion:** Site frame stays (header, nav, fonts, deep bg). Play area is a contained "felt table" panel with chases.house gold/ember accents on borders. The card palette deliberately pops against it.
- **The detail nobody asked for:** Idle on title screen >5s, two card-back rectangles slide across the felt and shuffle into the deck. Loops every 12s. Easy to miss — and that's the point.

## Resolved Decisions

1. **Online multiplayer:** **Deferred to v2 ADR.** No Firebase wiring in v1.
2. **Daily seed:** **Deferred to v2 ADR.** v1 still ships a seedable PRNG (Mulberry32) for forward-compatibility, but no UI surface.
3. **`SharedGamepad` extension:** **Moot — not required.** Verified during the architecture pass (`/feature-dev:feature-dev`, 2026-04-25): [games/shared/gamepad.js](../../games/shared/gamepad.js) lines 11–28 already export all 16 standard mapping indices (A, B, X, Y, LB, RB, LT, RT, BACK, START, LS, RS, DPAD_*). The "extend SharedGamepad" task is a no-op — Uno's `input.js` consumes the existing exports directly. Original ADR-016 policy ("the other 11 standard-mapping indices land when a consumer needs them") was apparently honored proactively.
4. **House-rule defaults:** **Stacking ON, jump-in OFF, 7-0 OFF, draw-then-play ON, force-play OFF, +4 challenge OFF.** Rationale: the casual-house feel most players expect (stacking + draw-then-play are the two house rules ~85% of households use), without surprise rule swings (jump-in, 7-0) or punitive enforcement (+4 challenge) that would confuse newcomers. Every default is a single toggle in the rules pane for purists.
5. **Voice "UNO!":** **Stinger only v1, vocal sample deferred to v2.** CC0 vocal samples in the right tone are scarce and licensing is fragile; a punchy synth sting carries the moment without sourcing risk.

6. **Audio implementation: procedural Web Audio synthesis (revised 2026-04-25).** Originally planned to fetch CC0 .ogg samples; revised during the audio implementation pass to fully procedural synth. Reasons: (a) the brief in §7 already endorsed synth for `chime-wild`, so extending it is consistent; (b) the graphic-poster aesthetic calls for chunky synthesized sounds, not realistic recordings; (c) clean CC0 sourcing is genuinely high-effort/risk per file × 15 files; (d) Pac-Man's analog isn't right (Pac-Man needs continuous music loops which are hard to synth — Uno is one-shot SFX only); (e) self-contained audio module = better long-term maintenance, zero asset weight. All 15 sounds in [games/uno/audio.js](../../games/uno/audio.js) are procedural. Run `UnoAudio.testAll()` in DevTools to hear them sequentially.
