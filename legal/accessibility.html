Accessibility

Last updated: May 12, 2026 · Standard targeted: WCAG 2.1 Level AA

Commitment

chases.house is a personal hobby site. I want it to be enjoyable to use for everyone, including people who use assistive technology, keyboard-only navigation, screen readers, or who have low vision, color-vision differences, or motion sensitivity. I aim for the site to meet Web Content Accessibility Guidelines 2.1 at Level AA.

What is in place

  • Primary text on the dark background passes WCAG 2.1 AAA contrast ratios.
  • The site respects the prefers-reduced-motion setting in your operating system — animations are dropped to near-zero duration if you have requested reduced motion.
  • All site fonts are self-hosted; no third-party font CDNs.
  • The site renders without JavaScript for everything except the interactive games themselves.
  • Keyboard navigation is supported for navigation, links, and most turn-based games (Chess, Sudoku, Crossword, Connect Four, Tic-Tac-Toe, Checkers, Yahtzee).

Known gaps

I am candid about what does not yet meet AA, in priority order:

  • Canvas-rendered games. Snake, Pac-Man, and Castle Tower Defense are drawn to an HTML canvas without programmatic state for screen readers. Right now they are not usable with a screen reader. Improving this is on the roadmap.
  • Buzzer audio cues in Jeopardy. The game uses sound to signal buzzer events. A visual (pulse / flash) alternative is in progress.
  • Focus indicators. Some game cells and buttons rely on the browser default focus ring. Higher-contrast, consistent focus styles are in progress.
  • Faint text. A few labels use a low-contrast “faint” color that passes AA only at large sizes. I am working through and replacing these where the size is small.

Assistive technology tested

The site has had informal testing with: NVDA on Firefox, VoiceOver on Safari (macOS and iOS), keyboard-only navigation, and the macOS Increase Contrast preference. It has not been tested with JAWS, Dragon, or every Android screen reader combination.

Telling me when something breaks

If you find an accessibility problem on the site — anything that makes it hard or impossible to use — please tell me. Even small reports help.

  • Email: chasej.house@gmail.com with the subject line “Accessibility”
  • If you can, include: the page or game, the browser and operating system, the assistive technology you were using, and what went wrong.

I will reply within a reasonable time and, where I can, fix the problem in the public commit history. If it is something I cannot fix quickly, I will tell you that too, so you are not left waiting.

Formal review

This site is not a commercial enterprise and is not a place of public accommodation under ADA Title III; I do not currently engage formal third-party WCAG auditors. That said, I treat reports the same way I would treat a commercial site's reports — they matter.
