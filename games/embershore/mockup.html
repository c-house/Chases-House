ADR-019 · DESIGN BIBLE V1

Embershore.

Sun-bleached dream-cartography. A small island someone has been carrying in their pocket for years.

01Palette

Six 4-color ramps anchored on the chases.house warm tokens. No new accent hues — only sub-shades within the warm-tone family, with cool ramps (water, stone, night) desaturated toward the warm bias. Site tokens noted in monospace; new shades are tinted from existing tokens.

Palette extension justification. Chases.house tokens give 5 anchor warm-tones (gold, glow, ember, terracotta, sage) and 4 UI grays. For 4-color GBC-style ramps per material I introduced 11 new shades, all derived from the warm bias: sand expansion uses two darker tints of ember; grass extends sage in both directions; stone is desaturated parchment; water is a warm-leaning blue (saturation ≤ 30%, hue rotated 4° toward cyan-warm). No new hues — only sub-shades within the existing warm family. --accent-ember appears in two ramps (sand and ember) deliberately so the palette feels of-a-piece rather than 6 disjoint pickers.

Why three fonts? A two-font system would force either the menus to feel diegetic (PSP for "Music: 60" looks like a dungeon HUD, breaking the menu/world boundary) or the in-world UI to feel papery (Fraunces hearts read as decorative, not as game state). Three fonts let each register hold its own integrity. DRY check: all three are already loaded by the chases.house site (Fraunces and Bricolage globally; Press Start 2P locally by Pac-Man). No new font requests.

04Title Screen

SURFACE 1 · TITLE
Embershore
a sleeping island, four sigils, one hearthwarden
a chases.house dream

WHAT'S LIVE

The sky breathes on a 40s hue-rotate loop. The Hearthwarden's cloud-silhouette pulses on an 8s ease (scale 1.00 → 1.04). Three wave-bands drift offset to suggest tide. The sun glows on a 4s alternate. The character bobs gently as if asleep.

THE WORD CHOICE

"Begin a dream" not "New Game." "Continue dreaming" not "Continue." The verbs commit to the conceit. If the player has no save, the second button shows as continue dreaming at 40% opacity, disabled.

WHEN A SAVE EXISTS

"Continue dreaming" reads the player's chosen name and shows it underneath: cinder, chapter 3 · the tide temple in Fraunces italic at 0.7rem.

FALLBACKS

Reduced motion: sky, clouds, hearthwarden, waves, sun, character all freeze. The player still sees a beautiful static frame.

NOT A MODAL — A LOGBOOK PAGE

The card has a page-curl shadow at the bottom-right corner, manifest-line at the top, ink fonts on parchment-tinted background. The conceit: the seabird that found you is making you a crew manifest entry.

QUICK-PICKS SHOW THE CHARACTER

Each pebble button renders a tiny silhouette of how the named character will look in-game — Finn in water-blue tunic (sea-coded), Cinder in terracotta (lore-tied to the Hearthwarden), Mavi in sage (forest-coded). Player picks an identity, not just a name.

DEFAULT → CINDER

Empty Enter selects "cinder" and continues. The placeholder text in the input shows "cinder" at 30% opacity to signal this. Pebbles toggle the input value; the input is the source of truth.

CHARACTER CONSTRAINTS

8 chars max, lowercased on entry, A–Z and space only. Strips emoji and special chars silently. If the player types something offensive the game still loads — we don't moderate. This is a personal site for cozy play.

APPEARS ONCE

Only on a fresh save, after the wash-ashore intro completes. Continuing a save skips this screen entirely. Starting a new game while a save exists prompts "overwrite the last dream?" first.

DECKLED PARCHMENT, NOT A RECTANGLE

The scroll has an irregular hand-torn top edge via SVG clip-path with 11 jagged points. Drop-shadow + inset paper-grain. The portrait is a circular frame with a 2.5px ink-colored border, NPC art inside, and a tiny name-stamp badge at the bottom in Press Start 2P at 0.45rem.

{NAME} INTERPOLATION

Whenever a string contains the player's name ({name} in source data), the rendered name is highlighted with a subtle ember-glow underbox — see "cinder" in the preview. Tells the player "this is yours." Implementation: dialogue.js string preprocessor wraps the substituted name in <span class="cursor-name">.

NPC-SPECIFIC FRAMING (V1 SUBSET)

The bard's box has a faint music-staff watermark behind the text. Totem-stone hints use a stone-carved frame (chiseled rectangular border) instead of parchment with a different font (Bricolage italic, slightly mystical). Fishermen and inn-keep use the default parchment shown here. Drop: per-NPC unique frames beyond these three categories — over-reach for v1.

TYPEWRITER

24ms per character. After a period, comma, or question-mark: +80ms. After a paragraph break: +400ms. Player can tap to skip the typewriter to instant-fill the box. Tapping again advances. The "▶" prompt at the bottom-right pulses on a 1.4s ease only AFTER the typewriter finishes.

HEIGHT

Box occupies bottom 28% of the screen. Game state below the box is paused (no enemies move during dialogue) — cozy, not punishing.

07HUD

SURFACE 4 · HUD (HOVER FRAME TO SOLIDIFY)
012

EMBER-BEAD HEARTS

Tiny radial-gradient orbs with a soft ember halo. Each heart pulses subtly (2.4s, staggered by 0.2s — a hearth-flicker). On damage, the bead fractures (diagonal crack via a clip-path overlay) and dims to --heart-empty. Lost-and-empty hearts become outline-only rings. Subverts the GBC pixel-heart trope while staying in palette.

RUPEE → "FOUND"

The currency is rupees but they're called "found" in NPC dialogue ("I'll take 5 found for that fishing line"). The icon is a gold-glow diamond with an inner ember-shadow. Numerals are zero-padded to 3 digits in Press Start 2P.

EQUIP SLOTS A & B

Two slots, each 22×22, with a tiny A/B badge in the corner showing the keyboard binding. The icon inside is a simple silhouette derived from the inventory item. Slots empty until items are picked up.

FADE-WHEN-FULL

Whole HUD at 70% opacity by default. Fades to 100% on any HUD-relevant event: damage taken, item picked up, slot swap. Holds at 100% for 3s, then eases back. Hover-to-solidify shown above demonstrates this. Player can tap H to permanently lock to 100% if they prefer.

RESERVED SAFE MARGIN

Top-left 15% of the screen is reserved as a HUD-safe zone. Dungeon room data flags the safe zone; enemies and pickups never spawn there. Guarantees the HUD never overlaps something the player must read or react to.

DOUBLE-PAGE FIELD JOURNAL

The pause menu is a hand-drawn journal spread, not a UI grid. Spine shadow runs down the center. Left page is the inventory grid; right page is the chart and stats. Background game state is blurred and darkened to 40% — present but distant.

EQUIP SLOTS BY GOLD-GLOW

Equipped items get a gold-glow background (rgba(232, 176, 74, 0.45)) and a tiny stamped A or B badge in the corner — the same letters that appear in the HUD. Player clicks an item, then presses A or B to assign it to that slot. Or arrow keys + Enter for keyboard.

STATS IN THE NPC LANGUAGE

"FOUND" not "rupees." "SIGILS" not "boss progression." "SHELLS" not "collectibles." Vocabulary commits to the lore. Why: reading "12 RUPEES" in the journal would break frame; "012 found" sounds like a sailor's tally.

MINI-MAP PREVIEW

A small parchment thumbnail of the overworld with the player's location pulsing as a gold ember. Clicking it opens the full Map screen (Surface 6). Always visible — players love seeing where they are.

SAVE BEHAVIOR

The game auto-saves on every screen transition (per ADR §Persistence), so "Save & Quit" is technically just "Quit." We label it Save & Quit anyway because cozy reassurance > technical accuracy.

FOLDED SEA-CHART, NOT A UI MAP

Three subtle vertical fold creases (33%, 66%) and one horizontal (48%) cross the parchment. Paper grain via radial-gradient pattern. Hand-drawn ink-line cell borders are dashed and slightly desaturated — they read as "drawn by hand" rather than "rendered by Tailwind."

HAND-DRAWN COMPASS ROSE

Top-right corner. Original SVG. Gold and ember on parchment. The dashed outer ring suggests cartographer's marginalia. Letters in Press Start 2P at 3px to keep the in-game font system consistent even on a story-tone surface.

CELL ICONS

Tiny glyphs say what's at each cell: ~ water, ▲ mountain, ⌂ village, 𓆟 fishing cove, ✦ dungeon (cleared, gold-glowing), ✧ dungeon (uncleared, faded), ◗ shell. The player's location pulses as an ember dot — the same heart-pulse animation as the HUD beads, for visual cohesion.

LEGEND IN THE NPC LANGUAGE

"Cleared / unfound / shell / you" — informal, lowercased. Not "completed dungeons" or "current position." Vocabulary stays cozy.

FOG-OF-MAP

Cells the player hasn't entered yet show no glyph (just dashed cell border). Dungeons appear as ✧ only after the player has been to the cell. Shells appear only after found. Encourages exploration; respects the cozy pace (no checklist anxiety).

10Game-Over · Wake in Inn

SURFACE 7 · WAKE IN INN
the dream blurs...
press any key to wake

NO DEFEAT LANGUAGE — ANYWHERE

Not "GAME OVER." Not "YOU DIED." Not even "Try again?" The screen reads "the dream blurs..." in Fraunces italic at 2.6rem with a soft ember text-shadow. Below: "press any key to wake" in lowercase Press Start 2P. That is the entire screen.

THE WINDOW

Above the line is an illustrated window — four panes of dawn-light gradient (ember → gold → terracotta → sand) with a subtle frost shimmer at the top. The implication: you are in bed, in the inn, looking at sunrise through the window. No words spell this out.

THE FADE

Slow 1.8s crossfade from the moment of damage. Music (the dungeon track) lowers in volume by -12dB across the same duration; the inn-theme then crossfades in over the next 2s. Player presses any key, the inn-theme stays, the screen transitions to the inn room interior (which is just the regular game with the player at full hearts, in the inn screen).

NO PROGRESS LOST

Per ADR. Dungeon enemies respawn. Items kept. Sigils kept. Found rupees kept. Hearts refilled. The screen is mood, not consequence.

WHEN IT FIRES

Hearts hit zero in any context (overworld, dungeon, fishing). The fade always shows. Even the boss fight uses this — bosses respawn at full health, but so do you.

LITERAL PALETTE INVERSION

Frame 1 (T=0): the in-game state at full warm palette — the cliff, sun-down sky, player on the beach. Frame 2 (T=6): brightness is dropping, the gradient is darkening, the player silhouette is dimming. Frame 3 (T=12): full inversion. Sky is now --night-4, the player and island silhouettes are now --accent-gold. The dream's afterimage.

THE FRAUNCES LINE

"and the hearthwarden dreamed of you" appears at T=8s and crossfades over the final 4s. Punctuation matters: lowercase, period at the end, no exclamation. It is a soft completion, not a triumph.

CREDITS UNDER

Below the line, in Press Start 2P at 0.4rem, the credits scroll in over the final 4s: "Embershore · a chases.house dream" — then names of CC0 asset creators (Patrick de Arteaga for music, Juhani Junkala for SFX, etc.) — then the ADR-019 link. Total credits ~10 lines. Player can press any key to skip after T=12.

WHY 12 SECONDS

Long enough to feel ceremonial. Short enough that someone watching over your shoulder doesn't get bored. The pacing matches the item-get pose's 2.4s × 5 — five item-gets worth of contemplation.

REDUCED-MOTION

Collapses to a 1.5s crossfade between Frame 1 and Frame 3. The Fraunces line still appears, the credits still scroll. The poetry survives even at instant speed.

13The Details Nobody Asked For

Small flourishes that reward a close look. None are mechanically required; all are mood-load-bearing.

THE IDLE LEAF

After 6s of no input on any screen — title, name-entry, gameplay, menu, ending — a single leaf drifts diagonally across the screen at a slight rotation. The leaf is 14×14px, --grass-2 on top, --ember-3 on bottom, with a subtle CSS-only shading. Drifts for 4.8s, rotates 720°, fades out at the right edge. Triggers once per idle period; doesn't repeat until the player moves the mouse or presses a key. (Live demo on this page — go silent for 6s.)

TUNIC FLUTTER

When the player character stands still for 6s, the tunic-edge sprite swaps to the "fluttered" variant for 200ms — a single frame showing the bottom-right hem lifted as if by a breeze. Same trigger as the leaf, deliberately synced — the leaf appears at the same instant as the flutter, so an observant player connects them: "the wind moved both."

MUSIC HOLDS THROUGH ROOM-SCROLL

Standard mistake: cut the music or fade it during a room transition. We don't. The music continues unbroken across the 280ms scroll. This is the single most load-bearing audio decision — without it, transitions feel like loading screens. With it, they feel like turning a page.

SIREN TRACK SHARES PALETTE WITH SKY

The Hearthwarden whisper track (a quiet ambient tone that plays at low volume across the whole island) modulates its own playback rate by ±2% on the same 8s ease as the Hearthwarden silhouette pulse. The world breathes in sync with itself. Most players never consciously notice; everyone feels it.

BARD'S VERSES REWARD FULL EXPLORATION

The bard NPC hums one bar of the final ritual song the first time you visit each new screen. Visit all 20 overworld screens and you've heard the full melody — assembled in your head, not on the soundtrack. The final ritual cutscene then plays the full track with vocals as the actual song. The reveal is that you already knew it.

TOTEM STONES KNOW WHAT YOU'VE DONE

Each totem checks game state and offers a contextual hint. Pre-first-dungeon: "the cove holds a quieter dream." After 2 sigils: "you wear two of her dreams now, {name}. she stirs." Before final ritual: "she is almost awake. light the campfire." Same totem, three lines over the playthrough. Players who re-read totems get rewarded.
